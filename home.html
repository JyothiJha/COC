ABOUT THE PROJECT

Diet is one of the most important factors that affect human health and well-being. However, many people struggle to maintain a healthy and balanced diet due to various reasons, such as lack of time, knowledge, motivation, or resources. Moreover, different individuals have different dietary needs and preferences, depending on their age, gender, lifestyle, medical conditions, and goals. Therefore, there is a need for a system that can help people plan and manage their diets according to their specific situations and requirements.
The system will allow users to create, modify, and track their personal diet plans based on their health goals. The system will also provide various features, such as calorie calculator for a day updated by the user and progress report. The system will use a relational database to store and retrieve information about foods, recipes, diet plans, and user profiles.

CALOIRES

A dietary calorie is defined as the amount of energy required to raise the temperature of one kilogram of water by one degree Celsius. We use the calories that we eat and drink for essential functions such as breathing and thinking, as well as day-to-day activities such as walking, talking, and eating. Calories are the amount of energy released when your body breaks down food. The more calories you consume, the more energy it can provide to your body. However, when you eat more calories than you need, your body stores the extra calories as body fat, leading to weight gain.
The simple answer is to maintain a healthy weight and overall wellness. When we eat and drink more calories than we use, our bodies store the excess as body fat. If this continues over time, we may put on weight. Knowing the calorie content of food and drink and how much we have makes it easier to keep track of our daily intake. Counting calories can help you eat fewer calories and lose weight. One review found that weight loss programs that included calorie counting led to an average of around 7 lbs(3.3 kg) more weight loss than those that didn't.

LUNCH

Lunch is a meal that provides energy and nutrients to keep the body and brain working throughout the afternoon. In the 17th century, lunch was a meal that could be eaten any time between late morning and mid-afternoon. However, during the 17th and 18th centuries, lunch gradually became a meal that was eaten later in the evening.
Eating a variety of foods in the proper proportions can help sustain health and vitality. Each type of food has a special nutritional value, and consuming a variety of foods boosts the immune system and helps fight against a range of diseases. According to Sharecare, you should eat every 2-3 hours. For example, you might have breakfast at 6 AM, lunch at 12 PM, and dinner at 6 PM.

DINNER

Dinner, traditionally the largest meal of the day, is a significant daily event across cultures. It's a time to unwind, nourish the body, and often, to gather with family or friends. The meal can be simple or elaborate, casual or formal, quick or leisurely. In many cultures, dinner features multiple courses, starting with appetizers, followed by the main course, and concluding with dessert. The dishes served can vary widely, influenced by regional cuisines, personal preferences, dietary restrictions, and seasonal availability of ingredients.
For some, dinner is a home-cooked meal enjoyed with family, a cherished ritual that fosters connection and a sense of belonging. For others, it might be a takeaway meal eaten in front of the TV, a quick bite at a local eatery, or a gourmet experience at a high-end restaurant. Regardless of its form, dinner is more than just sustenance. It's an opportunity to explore diverse flavors and culinary traditions, to celebrate special occasions, and to enjoy the simple pleasure of good food. In essence, dinner is a daily celebration of life's bounty and the joy of eating. Enjoy your meal!
